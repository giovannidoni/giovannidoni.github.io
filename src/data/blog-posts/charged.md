# Getting charged ⚡️
*How Zenvi happened*
 
It started with a few calls. Pablo and Manuel were pitching their new early stage startup in energy space and they wanted me on board as Head of LLMs. I knew one of them well. I knew what he was capable of. With the other I clicked with immediately. Together, I thought, we could cover a great deal of what a successful venture actually needs.

After the first couple of calls, I took a week off. End of April, the Camino del Norte, starting from Irun.

M and P happened to be there that same weekend. If it wasn’t for a nasty cough I’d picked up, we would have met there, nearly by chance, nearly inevitable. Delayed by one day.

First leg: Irun to San Sebastián. Thirty kilometres. Breathtaking views. And then the beach, that famous San Sebastián beach, with a sea-view beer and no mobile signal.

How come? It was 28th of April 2025: the blackout.

![San Sebastián Beach](/assets/charged.jpg)

5 days walking the Basque countries. In that moment, I also reconnected with something I’d half-forgotten: what Europe means. Not the headlines, not the numbers. Not the continuous beating of the public discourse. A way of life tested over centuries. I connected with the people. And something clicked that had been quietly forming for months: the parallel between Italy and Spain, two almost identical twins that could do so much together. Europe runs on two triads: wine, olive oil and sun in the south; beer, butter and wind in the north. A Southern European startup working on energy felt not just logical, but right.

The idea had deeper roots, going back further than I’d realised. From university. a friend was writing a thesis on battery storage…my own degree in materials engineering, nanotechnologies, electrolytic processes, the physics underneath... a PhD where energy is, literally, the main descriptor. The main token. Years later, reading the Newcleo prospectus - a company reviving fast neutron reactor technology - that spark reignited. Later on, I remember feeling that a balance sheet with little to no capex was something of the past, that you needed real weight to stay afloat in uncertain times.

All of it converging to: **let’s do it**.

That was a year ago. A year of building the business proposition followed. Testing, modelling, adjusting based on feedback. An ongoing process of learning, where AI itself became part of how we enriched and connected years of accumulated experience, and filled out the blanks spaces.

No revenue yet. Little to no pay.

But here we are. Ready to deploy.

Not just software. Energy storage.

In other words, **Zenvi**.
